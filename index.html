Traveling Salesperson Problem was first formulated in 1930 and is one of the most intensively studied problems in optimization. The Traveling Salesperson Problem describes a salesman who must travel between N cities. The order which he must travel does not matter as long as he visits each city once and finishes at the city he started. There is (N - 1 )! / 2 possibilities for N cities. To put that in context, there are 43,589,145,600 different possible routes for 15 cities.

Genetic Algorithm gives us a solution.
